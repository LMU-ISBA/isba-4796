Course Description

This course prepares students for the capstone experience by developing skills in business research methodology, proposal development, and project planning. Students learn to identify viable business problems, develop focused research questions, and create comprehensive capstone proposals. The course emphasizes practical application of research methods, analytical thinking, and professional communication skills essential for capstone success.

Course Format

The course follows a workshop format with four intensive sessions throughout the semester. Each workshop builds upon previous work, allowing students to iteratively develop and refine their capstone proposals through hands-on activities, peer feedback, and instructor guidance.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and define viable business problems suitable for capstone research
  • Develop focused research questions and project objectives
  • Conduct preliminary literature reviews and market analysis
  • Create comprehensive capstone project proposals
  • Design appropriate research methodologies and data collection plans
  • Present project proposals professionally to stakeholders
  • Apply ethical standards in business research and data collection
  • Establish realistic project timelines and deliverable schedules

How Are You Evaluated?

Final Capstone Proposal (50%) Comprehensive capstone project proposal with literature review, methodology, and implementation plan
Workshop Participation (25%) Active participation in all four workshop sessions and completion of workshop activities
Proposal Presentation (15%) Final presentation of capstone proposal to class for peer and instructor feedback
Peer Review & Feedback (10%) Quality of feedback provided to peers and engagement in review sessions

Course Policies

Attendance Policy

Regular attendance is essential for success in this workshop-based course. Students are expected to attend all four workshop sessions. If you must miss a session due to illness or emergency, please notify the instructor as soon as possible to arrange alternative participation methods.

Late Work Policy

Late submissions will be penalized 10% per day unless prior arrangements have been made with the instructor. Extensions may be granted for documented emergencies or health issues.

Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be original and properly attributed. Plagiarism, including the use of AI-generated content without disclosure, will result in course failure. Students are expected to uphold Loyola Marymount University's Academic Honesty Policy.

Technology Policy

Laptops and tablets are encouraged for note-taking and research activities. However, devices should be used only for course-related activities during class sessions.

Accommodations

Students with documented disabilities who may need accommodations should make an appointment with the instructor as soon as possible. Students should also contact the Disability Support Services (DSS) office to verify their eligibility for reasonable accommodations.
