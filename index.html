Course Description

Successful delivery of the Capstone Project requires students to have a thorough knowledge about the techniques and methodologies of user requirements gathering, systems analysis, design, test planning, and project management. This course will help students prepare for the Capstone course by introducing processes and tools to manage a complex IT project and deliverables. At the end of the course, students will have a completed and well-vetted Capstone Project proposal. By the time students begin the Capstone Project course, they will be well underway through the design and planning phase, allowing more time for implementation and execution.

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course students are expected to successfully meet the following capstone proposal development course learning outcomes:

  • Produce a well-vetted Capstone Project proposal
  • Understand the project stakeholders' needs and requirements
  • Identify project life cycle phases
  • Create data flow, use case, and entity-relationship diagrams to clarify requirements
  • Practical application of Agile project management processes with Scrum
  • Decipher when to use SaaS versus build from scratch
  • Know how and where to acquire hosting resources
  • Recognize the need for version control and be able to add, commit, and push changes to a repository

AI is a core part of this course and your future career

I describe myself as an AI optimist: I believe these tools will shape the future of work and create new opportunities, but I also know they can be misused if relied on without thought. In this class, AI is treated the same way employers are starting to treat it, as a tool, not a substitute for your judgment.

We will be using AI throughout the course. My goal is to help you learn how to use it effectively and responsibly, and to make your ability to use AI a skill differentiator that sets you apart in internships and job interviews.

‚úÖ You may use AI to:

  • Brainstorm ideas or outlines for your capstone project
  • Reformat, polish, or reword text you've already written
  • Automate routine tasks in your development workflow
  • Explore alternative approaches to problem-solving

‚ùå You may not use AI to:

  • Generate complete assignments, projects, or deliverables and submit them as your own
  • Avoid learning the underlying skills we're practicing
  • Hide your use of AI. Transparency matters

üìã Expectations:

  • If you use AI in your work, disclose it (for example, "I used ChatGPT to help me brainstorm project ideas")
  • I grade your understanding and ability to explain your process, not just the final answer. If you can't explain your solution without the tool, you haven't met the learning goal
  • Think of AI as a junior analyst ‚Äî useful, but still needs your oversight

üéØ Bottom Line

AI can give you leverage, but your critical thinking, decision-making, and ability to explain your work are what will make you stand out. Use this course to practice turning AI from a shortcut into a competitive advantage.
