Course Description

This course is designed to prepare students for the capstone experience by developing skills in research methodology, proposal writing, and project planning. Students will learn to identify viable business problems, conduct literature reviews, develop research questions, and create comprehensive project proposals. The course emphasizes critical thinking, analytical writing, and presentation skills essential for successful capstone completion.

Learning Approach

This course utilizes a workshop-based learning approach with hands-on activities, peer review sessions, and iterative feedback. Students will work individually and collaboratively to develop their research and writing skills through practical application and continuous refinement of their capstone proposals.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify and articulate viable business problems suitable for capstone research
  • Conduct systematic literature reviews using academic and industry sources
  • Develop clear, focused research questions and hypotheses
  • Create comprehensive project proposals with appropriate methodology
  • Present research proposals effectively to diverse audiences
  • Apply ethical considerations in business research design
  • Demonstrate proficiency in academic writing and citation standards
  • Collaborate effectively in peer review and feedback processes

How Are You Evaluated?

Proposal Development (40%) Iterative development of capstone proposal through multiple drafts and revisions
Literature Review (25%) Comprehensive review of relevant academic and industry literature
Research Methodology (20%) Development of appropriate research design and methodology
Presentations (10%) Formal presentation of proposal to class and feedback sessions
Participation & Peer Review (5%) Active participation in class discussions and peer review activities

Course Policies

Attendance Policy

Regular attendance is essential for success in this workshop-based course. Students are expected to attend all four workshop sessions. If you must miss a session due to illness or emergency, please notify the instructor as soon as possible to arrange alternative participation methods.

Late Work Policy

Late submissions will be penalized 10% per day unless prior arrangements have been made with the instructor. Extensions may be granted for documented emergencies or health issues.

Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be original and properly attributed. Plagiarism, including the use of AI-generated content without disclosure, will result in course failure. Students are expected to uphold the University of Colorado Boulder Honor Code.

Technology Policy

Laptops and tablets are encouraged for note-taking and research activities. However, devices should be used only for course-related activities during class sessions.

Accommodations

Students with documented disabilities who may need accommodations should make an appointment with the instructor as soon as possible. Students should also contact Disability Services to verify their eligibility for reasonable accommodations.
